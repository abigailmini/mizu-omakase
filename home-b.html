The Counter.

Dispatch · April 2026

Curved hinoki. Twelve seats. A single evening.

The counter was shaped from a single run of Japanese cypress, finished with no more than the oils of the chef's hands and a weekly pass of damp cloth. Its curvature was not an aesthetic decision — every seat must see every cut, every plate, every flash of flame.

There are no tables at Mizu. There is no private dining room. There is only the counter, lit from below, and the room around it that quietly recedes as the first course arrives.

店 内 · The Room

The market, twice a week.

Chef Howl flies to Toyosu every Tuesday and Friday, selecting fish the night before it is served. The boxes travel packed in ice to Kuala Lumpur within hours; by evening, they are on the counter. What arrives determines what is served — the reverse would be dishonest to the ingredient.

The remainder of the pantry is sourced from a small number of trusted Malaysian growers — rice from Kedah, chrysanthemum greens from Cameron Highlands, sakura salt we cure in-house from the season's blossoms.

The Experience · 本日の流れ

A sixteen-course
evening in movements.

i.
Movement I · Prologue

Sakizuke 先付

A cool greeting. Shirako dashi set with kuzu, spring herbs, the first breath of the evening at the counter.

v.
Movement V · Sashimi

Otsukuri お造り

Three cuts. Knife, blade, intention — translucent slices dusted in gold leaf for a single, deliberate pause in the evening.

ix.
Movement IX · Fire

Yakimono 焼物

Binchōtan. Skin crackles, flesh remains whole, rice still whispering warmth beneath. A movement of patience and heat.

xiv.
Movement XIV · Offering

Mukōzuke 向付

Earth and ember — a vessel that reveals itself only in the eating. The quiet, inward movement before the final breaths.

The Chef · 職 人

An unhurried
hand.

Chef Howl trained beneath traditional itamae in Tokyo and Osaka before returning to Malaysia to pursue his own expression of Edomae. His cuts are deliberate, his seasoning almost imperceptibly exact — a practice refined across two decades at the counter.

He has never used a timer in a kitchen. He is unlikely to tell you what you are eating unless you ask, and even then, he will tell you the fisherman's name first.

Before Your Visit · ご 案 内

A few notes for the evening.

01.

What is the price of the omakase?

＋
The evening menu is RM 688++ per guest for the full seasonal omakase (16+ courses). Seasonal supplements — truffle, caviar, wagyu — are offered at the counter.
02.

What is the dress code?

＋
Smart casual, with closed shoes. We kindly ask guests to avoid strong perfumes, as they interfere with the subtle seasonal aromas of the menu.
03.

How long is the experience?

＋
Approximately 2 to 2½ hours. Two seatings run nightly — 6:00 PM and 8:30 PM — Tuesday through Sunday.
04.

Dietary restrictions?

＋
Please let us know at least 48 hours before your reservation. We can accommodate most allergies; vegetarian and vegan omakase must be arranged in advance.
05.

Corkage & BYO?

＋
Corkage is RM 150 per bottle, limited to two bottles per party. Our own list features 14 sakes paired progressively with the courses.
06.

Cancellation policy?

＋
We hold a credit card at booking. Cancellations within 48 hours incur the full omakase charge, as ingredients are sourced specifically for your seat.
