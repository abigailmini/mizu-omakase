The Evening's Movements · 本日の流れ

The shape of an evening.

01
Prologue

Sakizuke

先 付
A cool greeting. Shirako-dashi set with kuzu, spring herbs, the first breath of the evening.
02
Soup

Wan-mono

椀 物
A clear dashi, twelve hours tended. Whatever the morning's harvest allows — bamboo, clam, or matsutake in its season.
05
Sashimi

Otsukuri

お 造 り
Three cuts, translucent. Wild sea bream, flounder aged two days, tuna the chef will name when he places it.
09
Grilled

Yakimono

焼 物
Binchōtan. A fish whose skin crackles, whose flesh remains whole, whose rice remembers the warmth beneath.
11
Nigiri Progression

Edomae Sushi

江戸前握り
Seven to nine pieces, each placed on the counter before you. Rice seasoned with aged red vinegar, built to the temperature of the chef's hand.
14
Final Savoury

Shime

〆
Hand-rolled temaki of toro and takuan, to close the savoury progression. A single, deliberate motion.
16
Dessert

Mizumono

水 物
Sakura-leaf ice cream, yuzu curd, a final note of the morning's cherry — the evening's signature, and its ending.
